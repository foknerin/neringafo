UX / UI

In UX Akademija we've worked on a group project called How to make a Sunday evening better? The target audience for the project's product were students, who arrived to the capital to study, has no job and still dependant on their parents. Our first task was to define the design persona, which helped us as a group to think in the same direction. The next task was to come up with the persona's experience map - a visualisation of how the persona is solving the problems at the moment. After that we had to engage with our target audience - we've prepared, planned and conducted the empathy interviews with people from our research segment. The results and insights received allowed us to define the persona's problem. We came up with a conclusion, that most of our persona's problems can be solved by finding a matching partner. First of all, the matching partner motivates, so that the study results improve. Secondly, having matching partner - getting desired close emotional relationship. Finally, time spent together is not limited to spending it in the clubs or bars, but can gain new forms and become more meaningful, purposeful and interesting. We've figured, that one of the most optimal ways to find a matching partner is using the dating app. Due to our results, that says, reliability and compatibility matters first, we offered the app, that encourages users to provide real data in their profiles. And the more information the user chooses to expose about himself - the more accurate profile matches are found. So our dating app invites users to be maximum honest and transparent to meet the same persons and to have respective relationships.
